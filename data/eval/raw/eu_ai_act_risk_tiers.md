# EU AI Act - Risk-based classification of AI systems

The EU Artificial Intelligence Act (Regulation (EU) 2024/1689) classifies AI systems into four risk tiers:

Unacceptable risk: these systems are prohibited and banned outright. Examples include social scoring by public authorities, manipulative AI that distorts behaviour, and untargeted scraping of facial images, with narrow law-enforcement exceptions for real-time remote biometric identification.

High risk: these face strict regulatory requirements such as risk-management systems, data governance, technical documentation, human oversight, and quality management. They include safety components of regulated products and the use cases listed in Annex III (critical infrastructure, education, employment, essential services, law enforcement, migration, and administration of justice).

Limited risk: these face lighter transparency obligations. Providers and deployers must ensure that end-users are aware that they are interacting with AI, for example for chatbots and deepfakes.

Minimal risk: these face no specific requirements and cover the majority of AI applications, such as AI-enabled video games and spam filters.

General-purpose AI models face graduated obligations, with additional rules for models presenting systemic risk, presumed when training compute exceeds 10^25 FLOPs.
