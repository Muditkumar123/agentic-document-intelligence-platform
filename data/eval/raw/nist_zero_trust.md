# NIST SP 800-207 - Zero Trust

Zero trust is a collection of concepts and ideas designed to minimize uncertainty in enforcing accurate, least privilege per-request access decisions in information systems and services in the face of a network viewed as compromised. Zero trust architecture (ZTA) is an enterprise's cybersecurity plan that uses zero trust concepts and encompasses component relationships, workflow planning, and access policies. Zero trust assumes there is no implicit trust granted to assets or user accounts based solely on their physical or network location.
